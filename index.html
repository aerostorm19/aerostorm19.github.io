About

Hi, I'm Abhijit. In India, friends call me "Abhi" — short and simple, like the code I try to write. I'm a 3rd-year Electronics & Telecommunication student at the Army Institute of Technology, Pune. I enjoy building things at the intersection of circuits, code, and curiosity — from crafting RISC-V CPU stages and SDR pipelines to designing fault-tolerant embedded controllers and ML-driven security tools.

Before university I pulled gadgets apart to see how they worked. That curiosity became engineering: STM32 control stacks for autonomous drones, FPGA SoCs, and large-scale hackathon systems. I like seeing small experiments scale into real impact — which is how GLOFzilla and ML-WAF came to life.

Outside engineering, I sketch, play basketball, and mentor juniors. I enjoy the mix of building, telling the story of what I built, and shipping it so others can use it.
